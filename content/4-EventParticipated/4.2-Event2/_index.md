# FCAJ Community Day - AI, CloudFront, LLM and Multi-Agent Systems

## Event Information

**Event name:** FCAJ Community Day - Speaker Sharing Session  
**Date:** May 23, 2026  
**Role:** Attendee

## Overview

This FCAJ Community Day brought together multiple speakers with different perspectives on learning systems, AI applications, cloud delivery, LLM fundamentals, hackathon execution, and enterprise AI architecture. Instead of focusing on one narrow topic, the event worked like a compact learning day where each session opened a new direction to explore.

The event was especially meaningful for me because it showed that cloud and AI are not isolated skills. A good engineer needs to organize knowledge, understand infrastructure foundations, evaluate AI behavior, and translate technical ideas into products that people can actually use.

## Main Sessions

- **Anh Tịnh - Build Second Brain:** Shared how to create a personal knowledge system so learning can be stored, connected, and reused over time.
- **Hải Anh - Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Q:** Introduced how AI assistants can support daily work and improve user interaction when they are connected to clear workflows.
- **Thịnh - From Edge to Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation:** Explained how CloudFront supports performance, reliability, security, and global delivery.
- **Team VIB - 36 hours with LotusHacks: Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality:** Shared lessons from turning an idea into a working product under hackathon pressure.
- **Đào Đức - Deep Dive Talk: How LLM Actually Works:** Explained how language models process context, predict output, and why users need to evaluate AI responses carefully.
- **Cát Vy - Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System: The Case of Startup Credit Scoring:** Presented how multiple AI agents can cooperate in a structured business workflow.

## Key Takeaways

### Personal Knowledge Management

The second-brain session helped me realize that learning becomes more valuable when notes are connected. Instead of storing information only for short-term use, I should build a system that helps me revisit ideas, link related topics, and reuse knowledge in reports, workshops, and projects.

### AI Assistants in Real Workflows

Amazon Q and AI assistant use cases showed that AI becomes useful when it is attached to a clear problem. A friendly assistant should understand user intent, provide reliable answers, and fit naturally into the workflow instead of acting like a separate tool.

### CloudFront and Edge Delivery

The CloudFront session reminded me that edge services are not only about caching static files. They also affect latency, availability, security, cost, and user experience. This topic helped me connect networking concepts with real product delivery.

### Product Building Under Time Pressure

The hackathon sharing showed the importance of prioritization. A short timeline forces the team to decide what matters most, divide work clearly, and communicate quickly. The story made me understand that a working product is often the result of many small but focused decisions.

### LLM Fundamentals and Multi-Agent Systems

The LLM and multi-agent sessions helped me use AI more responsibly. Understanding how LLMs generate answers makes it easier to identify hallucinations, ask better follow-up questions, and design systems where each agent has a clear role and boundary.

## Personal Reflection

This event gave me a broader view of how learning, cloud infrastructure, and AI system design connect with each other. The talks were not only technical; they also showed how engineers think, organize information, and communicate ideas.

After the event, I wanted to improve the way I manage notes and project knowledge. I also became more interested in CloudFront and multi-agent systems because both topics are practical for building scalable applications and AI-powered workflows.

## Networking and Community Value

- Learned from different speakers in the FCAJ community.
- Observed how technical topics can be explained through demos, stories, and product examples.
- Connected AI topics with AWS services and real-world use cases.
- Saw how community sharing can help learners discover new directions faster.

## Action Items

- Organize internship notes into a clearer second-brain structure.
- Review CloudFront and edge delivery concepts for future workshop content.
- Practice evaluating LLM answers instead of accepting them immediately.
- Study simple multi-agent patterns and how roles should be separated in an AI workflow.
