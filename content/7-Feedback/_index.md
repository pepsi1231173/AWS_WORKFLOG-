# Sharing and Feedback

After completing the internship in the **Workforce Bootcamp - First Cloud AI Journey** program, I would like to share my overall experience and feedback. This program gave me a valuable opportunity to learn about cloud computing, improve my documentation skills, participate in community activities, and understand how technical knowledge can be applied to a real project.

## Overall Evaluation

### Working Environment

The working and learning environment was professional, friendly, and supportive. The FCJ community created a space where interns could ask questions, share progress, and learn from each other. This helped me feel more comfortable when approaching new AWS topics and community activities.

### Support from Mentor and Team Admin

The mentor and team admin provided helpful guidance throughout the internship. When I had difficulties, I received suggestions that encouraged me to think and solve problems by myself before asking for direct answers. This approach helped me become more independent and improved my self-learning ability.

### Relevance to My Major

The internship was relevant to my major in Software Engineering. Although some AWS and cloud topics were new to me, they were connected to software development, system design, deployment, security, and documentation. This helped me understand how the knowledge from university can be expanded and applied in a cloud environment.

### Learning Opportunities

The program gave me many learning opportunities through worklogs, workshops, event participation, translated blogs, and project documentation. I had the chance to learn about AWS services, cloud architecture, AI-assisted development, DevOps concepts, and technical writing. These activities helped me build a more practical learning habit.

### Teamwork and Community

The teamwork and community spirit were positive. I could learn from teammates, speakers, mentors, and other community members. The events also helped me see how important communication and knowledge sharing are in the technology field.

### Internship Structure

The internship structure helped me keep track of progress through weekly worklogs and report sections. Having clear sections such as Worklog, Proposal, Blogs, Events, Workshop, Self-Assessment, and Feedback made the final report easier to organize and complete.

## What I Appreciated Most

What I appreciated most was the opportunity to learn from real community events and apply what I learned into my own report and project. The program did not only focus on technical knowledge, but also encouraged interns to write, reflect, communicate, and keep improving.

I also appreciated the support from mentors and teammates. Their feedback helped me improve the quality of my report and gave me more confidence when working with new topics.

## Difficulties Encountered

During the internship, I faced several difficulties:

- Some AWS concepts were new and required extra time to understand.
- Writing long technical content in both English and Vietnamese was challenging.
- Organizing screenshots, images, and report sections took a lot of attention.
- I sometimes needed guidance when solving technical or content structure issues.
- Communication outside my close group was still an area where I needed more confidence.

These difficulties helped me realize what I need to improve after the internship.

## Suggestions for Improvement

- Provide more short examples or sample structures for report sections at the beginning of the program.
- Create more small hands-on checkpoints so interns can verify their technical understanding earlier.
- Encourage interns to present short weekly updates to improve communication confidence.
- Provide a checklist for final report quality, including content, images, formatting, and bilingual consistency.
- Continue organizing community sharing sessions because they help interns learn from real experiences.

## Future Expectations

After this internship, I want to continue improving my cloud foundation, AWS hands-on skills, documentation ability, and communication skills. I also want to apply what I learned to future software projects, especially projects related to game development, backend systems, and cloud deployment.

Although I may follow my own career direction after the program, the knowledge and working habits I gained from FCJ will continue to support my learning path.

## Final Thanks

I would like to sincerely thank the FCJ team, mentors, speakers, and teammates who supported me during the internship. This was a meaningful experience that helped me grow not only in technical knowledge, but also in learning attitude, discipline, and professional awareness.
